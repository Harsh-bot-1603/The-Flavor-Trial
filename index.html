The Flavour Trail

A journey of flavours across India, stopping in Kolkata.

Bengali Cuisine

Aaheli – The Peerless Inn, Kolkata

📍 Esplanade / New Market area
💰 ₹2,000 for two
🕒 12:30 PM – 3:30 PM | 7:00 PM – 10:30 PM
👥 Top choice for authentic Bengali fine dining

Why it's the best:

🍛 Pioneering Bengali fine-dining since 1993—first of its kind in Kolkata.
🐟 Iconic dishes: Chingri Malaikari, Shorshe Ilish, Kosha Mangsho, Doi Ilish.
🍽 Royal thalis: “Rajbhoj” & “Mohatsav” include full-course Bengali meals.
🏛 Elegant Bengali décor and cultural atmosphere.
🌟 Critically acclaimed by chefs, locals, and food guides.

✅ Perfect for You If:

You want a refined, authentic Bengali meal experience.
Ideal for guests, celebrations, or heritage cuisine lovers.

North Indian Cuisine

Punjabee Rasoi – Kasba, Kolkata

📍 Rajdanga, Kasba
💰 ₹900 for two
🕒 11:00 AM – 11:30 PM (Daily)
👥 Most-loved for authentic Punjabi North Indian food

Why it's the best:

🥘 Top dishes: Mutton Adrak ke Panje, Dal Makhani, Malai Kulcha, Paneer Tikka.
🌟 Critically acclaimed & Reddit favorite — famous for bold, rich flavors.
🍽 Homely, no-frills setting with consistent taste & generous portions.

✅ Perfect for You If:

You want real Punjabi-style North Indian food without breaking the bank.

South Indian Cuisine

Banana Leaf – Southern Avenue, Kolkata

📍 Southern Avenue (near Lake Market)
💰 ₹300 for two
🕒 Open for lunch & dinner
👥 Top choice for authentic South Indian vegetarian food

Why it's the best:

🥘 Wide variety of dosas, idlis, vadas, and filter coffee.
🌿 Authentic flavors from Tamil Nadu, Andhra, Karnataka & Kerala.
💰 Affordable and consistently praised by locals and food critics.
🪑 Simple, clean ambience—great for casual dining.

✅ Perfect for You If:

You want delicious, value-for-money South Indian food in a relaxed setting.

Gujarati Cuisine

Shree Calcutta Gujarati Samaj (Vrajdas Dattani Bhawan)

📍 Bhowanipore, Kolkata
💰 Around ₹150–160 per thali
🕒 Lunch hours (typically 12 PM – 2:30 PM); Closed on Sundays
👥 Loved by the Gujarati community and food lovers alike

Why it's the best:

Authentic Gujarati thali with rotating menu: roti, shaak, dal/kadhi, 
rice, papad, pickle, and sweets like shrikhand, mohanthal, etc.

Feels like home-cooked food — simple, balanced, and deeply satisfying.

Community-run, not commercialized.

Extremely affordable and clean.

If you're looking for a true Gujarati experience, this is your best bet. 
It's not flashy — it's pure, soulful, and very Gujarati.

Rajasthani Cuisine

Rajdhani Thali – Park Street, Kolkata

📍 Park Street Area
💰 ₹500–600 per person
🕒 12:00 PM – 3:30 PM | 7:00 PM – 10:30 PM
👥 Top pick for unlimited, authentic Rajasthani thali

Why it's the best:

🍛 Lavish thali with 30+ Rajasthani & Gujarati dishes—unlimited servings.
🫓 Signature items: Dal Baati Churma, Gatte ki Sabzi, Bajra Roti, Kheer.
🏵 Elegant ambience with warm, traditional service.
🌟 Highly rated for authenticity, variety, and consistency.

✅ Perfect for You If:

You want a royal, all-you-can-eat Rajasthani meal with great hospitality.

Street Food

Kathi Rolls – Kolkata’s signature wrap (veg, chicken, egg, or mutton)
📍 Best Spot: Nizam's (New Market), Kusum Rolls (Park Street)

Puchka – Kolkata’s version of golgappa/pani puri; tangy and spicy
📍 Best Spot: Vivekananda Park, Southern Avenue | Vardaan Market

Ghugni Chaat – Spicy yellow peas with onion, lime & masala
📍 Best Spot: Dacres Lane, College Street

Telebhaja – Deep-fried fritters (beguni, alur chop, peyaji)
📍 Best Spot: Shyambazar 5-point crossing, Lakshmi Narayan Shaw (Hatibagan)

Momo (Tibetan-style dumplings) – Steamed or fried, veg or chicken
📍 Best Spot: Exide More (near Rabindra Sadan), Elgin Road stalls

Churmur – Crushed puchka, mashed potato, spices & chutney mix
📍 Best Spot: Vivekananda Park, Southern Avenue lakeside

Fish Cutlet / Kabiraji – Crispy, fried cutlets with spiced fish or chicken
📍 Best Spot: Allen’s Kitchen (Shobhabazar), Mitra Café
Sweet Treat: Mishti Doi & Rosogolla
📍 Best Spot: Balaram Mullick & Radharaman Mullick, KC Das
